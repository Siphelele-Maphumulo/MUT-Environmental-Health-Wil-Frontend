Protection of Personal Information (POPI) Act

The Protection of Personal Information Act (POPI Act), No. 4 of 2013, is South Africaâ€™s data protection law that seeks to promote the protection of personal information processed by public and private bodies. The Act sets conditions for the lawful processing of personal information to protect people from harm and uphold their right to privacy as guaranteed by the Constitution.

Key Aspects of the POPI Act:

  • Consent: Personal information may only be processed if the data subject consents to the processing, unless the processing is required by law or is necessary to protect a legitimate interest.
  • Purpose Specification: Personal information must be collected for a specific, explicitly defined, and lawful purpose related to a function or activity of the responsible party.
  • Further Processing: Further processing of personal information must be in accordance with or compatible with the purpose for which it was originally collected.
  • Information Quality: Responsible parties must ensure that the personal information is complete, accurate, not misleading, and updated where necessary.
  • Openness: Data subjects must be informed when their personal information is collected and must be made aware of the purpose for which it is collected.
  • Security Safeguards: The responsible party must secure the integrity and confidentiality of personal information in their possession or under their control by taking appropriate, reasonable technical and organizational measures to prevent loss, damage, or unauthorized access.
  • Data Subject Participation: Data subjects have the right to access their personal information held by a responsible party and to correct inaccurate or misleading information.

Compliance with the POPI Act is mandatory for all organizations that process personal information in South Africa. Failure to comply can result in penalties, including fines and imprisonment.

Application to Work Integrated Learning (WIL)

During your Work Integrated Learning (WIL) placement, you will be required to collect, process, and handle personal information. It is essential that you adhere to the principles of the POPI Act to ensure that this information is handled lawfully and ethically. This includes obtaining consent from individuals before collecting their personal information and ensuring that it is stored securely.

Responsibilities

By participating in the WIL program, you agree to comply with the POPI Act in all your dealings with personal information during your placement. You must ensure that any personal information you collect is necessary for your work, processed lawfully, and kept secure at all times.
